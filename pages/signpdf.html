Sign PDF online

Add a visible signature or approval mark to a PDF in your browser. It is useful for everyday approvals and informal document workflows.

Best for: internal approvals, forms, drafts, and quick sign-offs.
Input: PDF files.
Output: a signed PDF copy.

Before you download

This is not a certificate-based e-signature system. Use your required legal signing platform when compliance rules demand it.

How to sign a PDF

  1. Drag and drop your document into the signing area.
  2. Choose your signature type: Draw (using your mouse or touch screen) or Type (using a signature-style font).
  3. Position the signature on the page by clicking where you want it to appear.
  4. Click Apply & Download to save your signed document.

Frequently asked questions

Is this signature legally binding?

This tool provides a "wet signature" replacement (a visible mark on the document). While this is sufficient for many informal agreements, internal approvals, and basic forms, it does not provide cryptographic digital certificates (eIDAS/ESIGN compliant). Always check your local regulations for high-stakes legal documents.

Can I sign multiple pages?

Yes. You can navigate through the pages of your PDF and place your signature on any page you require before downloading the final file.

Are my signatures saved?

No. For your privacy, your drawn or typed signature only exists in your browser's session memory. Once you refresh or close the tab, the signature data is wiped.
